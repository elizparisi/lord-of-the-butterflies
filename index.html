Monarch Butterfly

King of Butterflies

The Monarch butterfly is one of the most recognizable species in North America. They are considered the king of butterflies, hence the name "monarch".

These butterflies go through four phases in their life cycle and four generations in a single year. You can learn more about the different stages here.

The fourth generation of Monarchs live longer and migrate to warmer climates like California and Mexico during winter months, traveling around 2500 miles! They rest through the winter and then return North in search of Milkweed.

The Monarchs' population is dwindling and they are considered to be on the verge of extinction. Both social and government agencies are working on initiaves to help grow the Monarch population.
